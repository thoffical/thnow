The Acueduct of Segovia
20 centuries ago
The Aqueduct of Segovia (Spanish: Acueducto de Segovia; more accurately, the aqueduct bridge) is a Roman aqueduct in Segovia, Spain. It is one of the best-preserved elevated Roman aqueducts and the foremost symbol of Segovia, as evidenced by its presence on the city’s coat of arms.

Dean Village
Dean Village (from dene, meaning ‘deep valley’) is a former village immediately northwest of the city centre of Edinburgh, Scotland. It was known as the “Water of Leith Village” and was the centre of a successful grain milling area for more than 800 years. At one time there were no fewer than eleven working mills there, driven by the strong currents of the Water of Leith. From Wikipedia
